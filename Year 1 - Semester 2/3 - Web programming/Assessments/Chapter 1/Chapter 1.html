Champorado is a sweet chocolate rice porridge that uses sweet glutinous rice (locally known as malagkit) and cocoa powder as main ingredients. A more traditional approach would be using tableya (pure cocoa blocks) instead of cocoa powder.

This porridge can be served hot or cold and is mostly eaten during breakfast. This can also be served during mid-afternoon snacks (meryenda)

Ingredients ‚úèÔ∏è

  1. 8 table spoons of cocoa powder or 4 pieces of tablea
  2. 1 cup of jasmine rice or sticky rice
  3. 1/2 cup of white or brown sugar
  4. 2 tablespoons (30 milliliters) water
  5. 3 1/2 cups of water
  6. Condensed milk (optional)

Instructions üìÉ

  1. Wash and rinse the rice once in your cooking pot.
  2. Pour in 3 1/2 cups of water and boil for 6 minutes.
  3. Add in your 8 table spoons of cocoa powder or 4 pieces of tablea, stir until the cocoa is diluted.
  4. Add in your 1/2 a cup of sugar, let it boil for another 6 minutes until the texture runs thick.
  5. Serve in a bowl and add a swirl of condensed milk
