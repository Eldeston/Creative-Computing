# Day 1 Activities
Winner: 1 Team
Team Count: 4
Team Members: 5
Rewards : 15 medals (5 each game, 1 each member), Certificate of Recognition
## Cyber Lockdown: 45 Minutes
**Description:**
Teams solve cybersecurity-themed puzzles to “escape” a simulated cyber attack.

**Mechanics:**
- Each team receives clues related to phishing, malware, encryption, and safe browsing
- Correct answers unlock the next clue
- The first teams to complete all puzzles win

**Materials Needed:**
- Printed puzzle sheets
- Envelopes with clues
- Timer
- Whiteboard

**Manpower:**
- 2 Game Marshals
- 1 Timekeeper
## Password Relay: 40 Minutes
**Description:**
A physical and mental relay game focusing on password security and best practices.

**Mechanics:**
- Each team member completes a short physical task
- After each task, the player answers a password-related question
- Incorrect answers send the team back to repeat the task

**Materials Needed:**
- Cones
- Password question cards
- Whistle

**Manpower:**
- 2 Facilitators
- 1 Scorekeeper
## Digital Forensics: 30 Minutes
**Description:**
A scavenger hunt where teams investigate a simulated cybercrime.

**Mechanics:**
- Teams search for hidden QR codes around the venue
- Each QR code reveals a digital clue
- Teams analyze clues to identify the “hacker” and explain the incident

**Materials Needed:**
- Printed QR codes
- Smartphones (students’ own)
- Answer sheets

**Manpower:**
- 3 Facilitators
- 1 Technical Assistant

## Robot Making (Arts & Crafts)
Participants: Individual or group (maximum 3 members)
Rewards: 3 trophies, 1 Gift Card (AED 20), Certificate of Recognition, Certificate of Participation

**Description:**
An open creative activity for students passionate about innovation, design, and robotics. Participants will design and build a robot prototype or concept that solves a real-world problem. Announce the competition 2 weeks prior to participants for preparations. Participants will buy their own materials.

**Format:**
- Open to all students (individual or group)
- Designs may be:
- Physical models (recycled materials, basic kits)
- Concept boards, sketches, labeled diagrams

**Output & Display:**
- All robot designs will be displayed along school hallways as a Technology & Innovation Exhibit

**Judging Criteria:**
- Creativity and originality
- Design and functionality
- Problem-solving concept
- Presentation and explanation

**Awards:**
- 3 Official Winners
- Cash prize OR
- Additional academic marks (subject to approval)

Team Count: 4
Team Members: 5
Prizes: 5 Medals, Certificate of Participation

**Description:**  
The call of duty Scrim is a friendly yet competitive tournament among the players. This scrim aims to strengthen camaraderie, promote teamwork, and showcase members’ gaming skills in a fair and exciting competition.

**Game Mode:**  
Each match will be played in Call of Duty: Mobile – Search and Destroy (S&D). 

**Qualified players:**
Open to all Metaverse Computing, Cybersecurity, and IFS students across all levels. However, only 20 players will be selected to compete in the main event.
### Format
**Elimination Round**
- Round 1: Group 1 vs Group 2
- Round 2: Group 3 vs Group 4

**Finals**
- Round 3: Winner of Round 1 vs Winner of Round 2

1. No cheating, exploiting, or use of unauthorized third-party software

3. All matches must be played live with no replays unless technical issues occur
4. Team captains must report match results immediately to the event coordinator

## Debug Dome: Python Cyber Debug Challenge
Participants: Individuals (maximum 15 individuals), Creative Computing, Cybersecurity
Prizes: 2 Trophies, 1 Gift Card (AED 20), Certificate of Participation

DEBUG DOME is a cybersecurity-themed Python debugging competition where students act as Cyber Defense Engineers tasked with fixing vulnerable and broken Python programs before a simulated cyber attack causes system failure.

Instead of writing code from scratch, participants must analyze, debug, and secure existing Python programs, making the challenge accessible to both 1st and 2nd year students while still testing logical thinking, syntax knowledge, and cybersecurity awareness.
### Objectives
The competition aims to:
- Develop debugging and problem-solving skills
- Strengthen understanding of Python fundamentals
- Introduce secure coding practices
- Enhance teamwork and analytical thinking
- Simulate real-world cybersecurity scenarios
### Competition Format
- Type: Individuals
- Language Used: Python
- Duration: 40 minutes
- Difficulty Level: Progressive (Easy → Medium → Security-focused)
### Scoring System

| Criteria               | Points  |
| ---------------------- | ------- |
| Syntax Bug Fixed       | 10 pts  |
| Logic Bug Fixed        | 15 pts  |
| Security Improvement   | 20 pts  |
| Code Runs Successfully | 20 pts  |
| Clean & Readable Code  | 10 pts  |
| Total Possible         | 100 pts |
Time bonus: +10 pts for early completion

### Materials & Requirements
**For Participants:**
- Laptop with Python installed
- Code editor (VS Code / IDLE / PyCharm)

**For Organizers:**
- Printed problem sheets
- USB / Google Drive access to files
- Timer
- Rubrics
